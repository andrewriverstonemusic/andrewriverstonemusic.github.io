The 10 tracks making up Andrew's 5th solo album delve deeper into influences
from blues/rock pioneer guitarists Paul Kossoff, Peter Green and Jimmy Page,
who inspired Andrew to learn to play. Elsewhere the songwriting and vocal influence
from soul legend Al Green, American troubadours Neil Young and Tom Petty can be heard.
Andrew's trademark sound of blues riffery over African percussion is ever present
with inventive songcraft telling stories ranging from an infamous 1960s cornish sea tragedy,
to a tribute London eccentric live music superfan Jesus Jellett.
(As seen in the Rolling Stones Rock & Roll circus film, and Cream's farewell concert at the Albert Hall).

'Out On The Money Go Round' features recently re-discovered archive audio
of Andrew jamming with Talking Heads/ Albert king bassist Busta Jones,
put to a new song with lyrics about an imbalanced worklife balance.

Bleak thoughts following a relationship breakup fuelled the driving blues/rock
'Pushing Against The Flow' and the moody swing blues of 'Shades Of Blue (Highwire)'.
Andrew shows a lighter side and wry sense of humour on the slinky riff & beat of
'Back In The Cool House','Out On The Money Go Round' and the driving swing/rock of
'He's The Richest Man'.

'Ancient Valley Of The Rocks' is a haunting guitar instrumental tuning into the majestic
atmosphere of one of Andrew's favourite sites in North Devon and pays tribute to Peter Green.
